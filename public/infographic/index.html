EAT FUCK GO

Every organism faces one budget with two demands: EAT — survive and out-compete — versus F*CK — reproduce and out-multiply. This game is a duel between those two forces of evolution, decided by terrain, a rolled scenario, and the dice.

🦷 EAT IT — acquire resources
🧬 F*CK IT — propagate genes
The core idea

Finite energy, two strategies

Life-history theory in a card game: energy spent eating can't be spent breeding. Each round you split your energy on a slider — 🦷 Fight turns energy into combat dice, 🧬 Breed turns it into life and offspring. Pour it all into winning the clash, or invest in a swarm that outlasts your rival. That tension is the whole game.

A round, step by step

How the story plays out

①

Terrain sets the stage

You fight over one biome for the whole battle. It buffs its natives and bends every rule (blocks rangestrike, negates ambush, and more).

②

Scenario is rolled

Players take turns rolling 1d6 for a transient condition — abundance, darkness, crowding… — that tilts this round's dice.

③

Choose your strategy

Taking turns, each player deploys the card best suited to this terrain-scenario combo. Preview it, swap freely, then commit.

🎲

Resolve with dice

Each strategy rolls a pool of dice; whoever lands more hits wins the clash. The loser drops a life.

The clash

How a face-off is decided

A card's Power becomes its number of dice; its ADA (adaptability) sets how easily each die hits. Terrain, scenario, catastrophe and keywords then add or remove dice. Both sides roll — most hits wins.

Keyword counters

Biological superpowers

Some strategies carry a trait that beats or breaks others — the game's rock-paper-scissors.

Terrains

The stage bends the rules

Where you fight changes who wins. Each biome buffs its natives and enables or disables whole tactics.

Scenarios

The moment's demand (rolled each round)

A 1d6 scenario is the round's twist — it rewards some strategies and punishes others.

Catastrophes

Round 3: roll matched dice, and the world changes

At round 3 both players roll a die. Match the number and a catastrophe strikes — a global upheaval that warps the rest of the battle, often flipping which strategies win.

The strategy decks

Every strategy card

Attackers pick a deck; both sides field only that deck. In an EAT IT battle a card's OFF is its Power (how many dice it throws); in a F*CK IT battle its REP is.
