EAT FUCK GO

A 2–4 player duel of evolutionary strategies across the biomes of a single mountain — after Alexander von Humboldt's Naturgemälde. Every organism faces one budget with two demands: EAT (survive & out-compete) versus F*CK (reproduce & out-multiply). Wander the map settling niches, and clash where your frontiers meet — as the climate warms.

🦷 EAT — acquire resources
🧬 F*CK — propagate genes (anglerfish: male fused to female)
The goal

Control the most biomes

The mountain holds 10 biomes, each split into hex "patches." You control a biome only when you hold every patch of it. Grab a majority of the living biomes and you win outright — but in a crowded 3–4 player game that's rare, so the match otherwise ends when the planet hits +4 °C, and whoever controls the most biomes then wins (ties broken by hexes).

Each player picks a legion emblem at the start — a Japanese-fireworks shell (🎆 chrysanthemum · peony · kamuro ring · willow) in their colour. It blooms over every hex you hold and brightens when you own a whole biome, so you can read the map's balance of power at a glance.

The board

A mountain of niches

The board is Humboldt's Naturgemälde — biomes stacked by climate from the sea to the summit, laid out as a mirror-symmetric hex field so no one has an edge. The 2–4 players start spread apart — the two ocean corners, then the summit vent, then the central cave — and wander outward. The two GM Labs sit dead-center.

Naturgemälde (1807), A. v. Humboldt & A. Bonpland — Zentralbibliothek Zürich · public domain. The game board overlays this cross-section with hex biome patches.

Each biome supplies elements (sunlight, water, minerals, meat, plants, hosts) and favors one strategy — 🦷 EAT (predation & energy) or 🧬 F*CK (proliferation & speciation). Strategies suited to a biome's elements roll more dice there; the favored faction rolls an extra die.

On your turn

Roll to move, then muster or clash

Movement is Titan-style: your die sets how far you can reach, and what you do depends on who is on the hex. You only fight where two players' frontiers actually meet.

🎲

Roll 1d6 to move

Your roll is your reach — you may act on any hex within that many steps of your territory. Roll 1 = neighbours only; a big roll lets a lineage disperse across the map.

🌱

Muster a neutral hex

A free claim, no fight. You settle the niche and recruit one strategy from that biome's EAT/F*CK ladder into your arsenal — build each ladder from the bottom up. Mustering does not heat the planet. See every biome's recruit ladders →

⚔️

Clash a rival's hex

Attacking a hex a rival holds starts the full clash (below). Win and you take it. Only clashes warm the planet.

↻

Turn passes

Play rotates to the next player, who rolls their own movement die.

The contest

Strategies → species → a champion

EAT and F*CK aren't species — they're the two allocations every organism makes. A strategy is an abstract way to eat or breed (chemosynthesis, pack hunting, broadcast spawning…). A species is a real organism that carries a strategy in a particular biome. Each clash, your in-hand strategies map to the species that live in the contested biome:

①

Attacker picks a mode

EAT or F*CK for this biome. You lean your team's way but aren't locked to it.

②

Name a dominant species

Your strategies map to the species that carry them here — you pick one to lead the clash (+2 dice).

③

Defender answers

They see your champion & roster, then concede the hex or name their own dominant species.

🎲

Clash!

The two champions face off (below), other strategies still fight — no bonus.

Every biome offers both EAT and F*CK species, so the mode you choose changes which champions are on the table:

The clash

How a face-off is decided

A strategy's Power becomes its number of dice — in EAT mode Power is a card's OFF, in F*CK mode it's REP. Its ADA (adaptability) sets how easily each die hits. Then the biome, the round's scenario, and your dominant-species pick add or remove dice. Both sides roll each round; the loser drops a life; run out and your champion dies.

Power → 🎲 dice
OFF (EAT) or REP (F*CK)
ADA → hit on N+
more adaptable = easier hits
🏞️ Biome fit
+1 die per matched element; +1 for the favored faction
👑 Dominant species
your champion: +2 dice — eroded by adaptation
🍩 Donuts = life fighting in EAT mode
🥚 Eggs = life fighting in F*CK mode
Most hits wins the clash; life to 0 loses the battle.

🔴 The Red Queen. Lean on a strategy and the world adapts to it: each time you lead a clash with a strategy, its champion bonus drops next time — +2 → +1 → 0 → −1 — while strategies you rest recover. Spam one ace and it turns into a liability; a diverse repertoire stays sharp. The species picker shows each champion's current standing ("fresh · champ +2" → "adapted ×2 · champ 0").

🌀 Weirdos. If a clash drags to round 3+, either fighter may summon a weirdo — roll 1d6 to pull one of six oddball lineages (Lamprey, Platypus, Horseshoe Crab, Fungi Network, Tardigrade, Mantis Shrimp), each bringing a fresh 3-card stack into the fight. They aren't owned or collected — they're a shared wildcard, one summon per side per clash. Browse all six in the Hand panel.

Each round of a clash

The moment's demand (rolled 1d6)

Every round of a clash, a player rolls a scenario — a transient condition that rewards some strategies and punishes others. Read it before you deploy.

Your collection

Grow your roster — and watch species go extinct

You start with a small team-biased collection and can only field species you own — so which niches you settle and which clashes you win shapes what you can bring to the next fight:

🌱 Settle
muster a biome → recruit one strategy up its EAT/F*CK ladder
🧬 Absorb
win a clash → the defeated dominant species joins your collection (your only path to off-team species)
🦴 Extinction
warming erases a biome's last patch → its species leave the game for everyone

🎴 Your hand. The Hand button on the board opens your stack any time — your species (grouped by biome), the strategy arsenal they carry with each champion's Red Queen fatigue, and the shared weirdo pool. In pass-&-play you can tab over to peek at any rival's hand too; in future online play each hand stays private to its owner.

The warming isotherm

Conflict heats the planet

Humboldt mapped the world's isotherms. Here, temperature climbs from 0.0 °C to +4.0 °C, gaining +0.5 °C for every 3 hexes won in a clash — peaceful mustering stays cool, so the planet only warms once players start fighting over borders. As it rises, the most heat-vulnerable biomes transform, and whoever held a transformed hex is displaced (it goes neutral), forcing migration. At +4 °C the match ends and the biome leader wins.
