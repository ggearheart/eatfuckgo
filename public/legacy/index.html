STEP 0 — CHOOSE YOUR LINEAGE

BEFORE THE GAME BEGINS
Each player selects a lineage — the evolutionary branch their agents descend from. Lineage determines your starting strategies, stat spread, starting terrain, and a persistent special ability that lasts the entire game. See the Choose Lineage tab for full details on each path.
THE SIX LINEAGES
🦅 Birds — MOV 6, Aerial Survey
🐘 Mammals — ADA 6, Endothermy
🦠 Microbes — REP 10, Ubiquity
🐸 Amphibians — REP 6, Dual Life
🦖 Reptiles — DEF 5, Solar Efficiency
🐟 Fish — REP 8, Lateral Line
WEIRDO VAULT (OPTIONAL)
Instead of choosing a standard lineage, a player may roll 1d6 and be assigned a Weirdo organism (Lamprey, Platypus, Horseshoe Crab, Fungi, Tardigrade, or Mantis Shrimp). Weirdos have extreme stat spreads and unique rule-breaking abilities. Intended as a challenge run or for experienced players only.
LINEAGE LOCKING
Your lineage is permanent. Special abilities cannot be lost, stolen, or disabled by strategy cards, combat, or Catastrophe events. They represent deep evolutionary heritage — not a strategy card.

STARTING SETUP

AGENTS
Each player places 2 agents on their lineage's preferred starting terrain hex. Agents begin with their lineage's starting EAT IT and F*CK IT strategies (see Choose Lineage tab). All agents of the same lineage are identical to each other at game start.

DECKS
Three decks: EAT IT (21 cards), F*CK IT (20 cards), CATASTROPHE (20 cards). Shuffle each separately. Catastrophe deck is face-down in center of board. Starting strategy cards are taken directly from the relevant decks before shuffling.

TURN STRUCTURE

1 · MOVE
Move each agent up to MOV hexes. Entering a new terrain hex triggers a terrain event.
2 · TERRAIN EVENT
Draw from EAT IT or F*CK IT deck based on the hex's icon. If you meet the terrain + prerequisite requirements, you may keep the card. Otherwise discard.
3 · OPTIONAL: BATTLE
If two agents occupy the same hex, the occupying agent may challenge. Attacker declares EAT IT or F*CK IT battle type. Each side uses only that deck's cards. Resolve on the Battle Board. Winner adapts (draws 2 cards, keeps 1). Loser loses 1 surviving card of winner's choice. If all cards are eliminated, the agent dies — trigger a Spawn Check.
4 · SPAWN CHECK (after agent death only)
When an agent dies, the losing player immediately checks their highest REP value held across all surviving agents. If sufficient, they may spawn a replacement next turn at their lineage's starting terrain with base starting strategies only. See Spawn table on the Agent Death card.
5 · CATASTROPHE CHECK
If any player completed a full lap this turn, flip the top Catastrophe card. All agents respond simultaneously. Roll 1d6 + ADA for each agent. Fail threshold is printed on the card.

STRATEGY RULES

HOLDING LIMIT
Each agent may hold max 4 strategy cards total (EAT + F*CK combined). Must discard down if over limit.
SPECIATION
After surviving 3 terrain events, an agent "speciates" — draw 2 upgrade options from the relevant tier deck. Choose 1. The replaced card goes to discard.
MUSTER RULE
You can only acquire a strategy card if: (a) your agent is in the required terrain hex, AND (b) you already hold all listed prerequisite strategies.
CROSS-DECK CO-REQUISITES
Some strategies require cards from the other deck. Pack/Cooperative Hunt (EAT) requires a social F*CK strategy. Eusociality (F*CK) requires Pack Hunt (EAT). These dependencies model real evolutionary constraints.
CATASTROPHE SURVIVAL ROLLS
Roll 1d6 + ADAPT modifier. Result determines survival. Certain strategy cards grant immunity or bonuses. Spore/Cryptobiosis grants one-time full immunity per game.

WIN CONDITION — ELIMINATION VICTORY

LAST LINEAGE STANDING
The last player with at least one living agent on the board wins. There are no point tracks, no territory scores — only survival. Every decision is a trade-off between growing stronger (EAT IT) and staying in the game longer (F*CK IT).
AGENT DEATH
An agent dies when all of its strategy cards are eliminated in battle, or when it fails a Catastrophe survival roll with ADA below the event's threshold. A dead agent is removed from the board immediately. Its cards are discarded — not returned to the deck.
PLAYER ELIMINATION
A player is eliminated when they have no living agents AND insufficient REP to spawn a replacement (see Spawn table). Eliminated players become Ghost Lineage — they may advise any active player once per round but take no actions.

SPAWN RULES
Spawned agents start with lineage base strategies only — no accumulated cards carry over (except the 1 inherited card at REP 11+). Spawned agents may not battle on the turn they are placed. REP 1–4 players are permanently eliminated if they lose their last agent.
K vs R STRATEGIST DYNAMIC
R-strategists (Microbes REP 10, Fish REP 8, Amphibians REP 6) lose agents freely and replace them fast — they win by attrition and board flooding. K-strategists (Reptiles REP 2, Birds REP 4, Mammals REP 3) are hard to kill individually but cannot easily replace losses — guard each agent obsessively.

STAT REFERENCE

OFF  OFFENSE
Attack power in direct combat. Also used for some terrain challenge rolls.
DEF  DEFENSE
Resistance to attack and environmental hazards. High DEF slows but protects.
REP  REPRODUCTION
Your lineage's spawn capacity. When an agent dies, your highest REP value determines whether you can replace it and how fast (see Agent Death & Spawn card). REP 1–4 = no spawn. REP 5–7 = 2-turn delay. REP 8–10 = next turn. REP 11+ = free + inherit one card.
ADA  ADAPT
Survival modifier for Catastrophe rolls. High ADAPT = survive extinction events. The most important stat for long games.
MOV  MOVEMENT
Hexes traversable per turn. Determines how fast agents can muster new strategies or escape threats.

SPECIAL TOKENS

APEX TOKEN
Awarded to any agent holding the Apex Crushing Predator card. All other agents must spend +1 MOV to enter or exit that hex. Cannot be eliminated except by Severity 4+ Catastrophe cards.
VENOM TOKEN
Placed on a target agent by a Venom Injection holder. Each round the token remains, target loses 1 OFF. Target must spend 1 action in a safe hex to remove it.
TRAP TOKEN
Placed on an adjacent hex by a Trap Building holder. Any agent entering that hex without MOV+4 or higher takes OFF-2 this round.
INVASIVE APEX TOKEN
Spawned by the Apex Invader Catastrophe card. Appears in all hexes. Requires combined OFF 12+ from multiple players in same hex to remove.
